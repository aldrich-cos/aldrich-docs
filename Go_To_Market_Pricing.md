# Aldrich — Go-to-Market & Pricing Strategy

**Document purpose:** Define how we get Aldrich into the hands of the right users, in what order, at what price, through what channels — from the first 100 users to the first 100,000.

**Stance:** Most consumer AI products die from poor distribution, not poor product. We treat GTM with the same rigor as engineering — owned KPIs, testable hypotheses, weekly review.

---

## Executive summary

**GTM thesis:** Aldrich earns users by delivering visible, daily value to a narrow but high-intensity persona (the multi-threaded executive). They become organic promoters because the product makes their work life visibly better. We amplify word-of-mouth with targeted content distribution, founder-led PR, and a referral mechanic. We avoid paid acquisition until unit economics are proven.

**The 0-to-100K path:**

1. **0 → 100 users** (pre-launch waitlist + hand-picked beta): high-touch, curated, product-focused.
2. **100 → 1,000 users** (closed beta): referral-only; tight feedback loops; early testimonials captured.
3. **1,000 → 10,000 users** (public launch): Product Hunt, HN, Twitter, press, podcast circuit; referral program active; first content flywheel spinning.
4. **10,000 → 100,000 users** (growth phase): content SEO, creator partnerships, community, B2B prosumer (Maya brings it to her team).

**Pricing architecture:**

- **Free** — core memory + briefings + 5 actions per day. Designed to hook, not to fully satisfy.
- **Pro — $18/month or $180/year** — unlimited actions, all integrations, premium support.
- **Power — $35/month or $340/year** — power-user features (custom automations, team memory, API beta).
- **Annual default** (discount framed as "2 months free"), monthly available.

**Target conversion funnel at maturity:**

- Sign-up → 48h active: 70%
- 48h active → Week-1 habitual: 55%
- Week-1 habitual → Paid trial start: 25%
- Paid trial → Paid: 55%
- **Overall sign-up → paying: ~5.3%**

## Part 1: The stage-by-stage GTM plan

### Stage 1: Pre-launch (now → Week 17)

**Goal:** Build a 10,000-person waitlist of qualified, intent-signaling users before public launch.

**Audience filter on the waitlist form:**

Simple 3-question sign-up form on the landing page:
1. Your role (dropdown: exec / manager / IC / founder / consultant / creator / other)
2. Your biggest time-drain (free text, optional)
3. Your email

Question 1 lets us segment invites by persona fit in beta. Question 2 gives us real-language material for messaging and content.

**Channels:**

- **Founder narrative on X and LinkedIn.** Weekly posts from the CEO and CTO about building Aldrich — the problem, the vision, tradeoffs. Builds parasocial trust. Target: 1K followers each by launch.
- **Targeted newsletter placements.** Three buy-in opportunities: Lenny's Newsletter (product people), The Generalist (operators/execs), Every (knowledge workers). Small-scale sponsored slots to drive waitlist signups.
- **Podcast circuit — founder-focused.** CEO on 10–15 podcasts targeting our personas: product, operations, productivity, AI-for-work. Each generates 200–2,000 waitlist additions if well-matched.
- **Private Slack / Discord communities.** Seed conversations in high-signal communities (Superpath, Lenny's Community, On Deck, Reforge alumni). No spam; value-first participation with occasional mention.
- **A public "build log" or "Aldrich notes" page.** Weekly build updates shared via email and RSS; becomes the content flywheel for months to come.

**Metrics for Stage 1:**

- Waitlist size: 10,000 by Week 17
- Waitlist quality (Q1 breakdown): 40%+ exec/manager/founder fit
- Engagement: 30%+ email open rate on waitlist updates
- Community signal: referenced in at least 3 high-signal external conversations per month

---

### Stage 2: Closed beta (Week 17 → Week 20)

**Goal:** 200 → 5,000 active beta users with strong feature completion, retention signal, and early testimonials.

**Invite strategy:**

- Week 17–18: 200 invites to hand-picked waitlist (best-fit personas, thoughtful answers)
- Week 18–19: 1,000 invites prioritizing Maya-type execs and strong referral generators
- Week 19–20: Open to 5,000 with referral mechanic ("skip the waitlist by inviting 2 people")

**What we do during beta:**

- Weekly founder email ("week 2 of beta — here's what we shipped")
- In-app feedback capture tied to specific actions
- Monthly 30-min user interviews with 5–10 users/week
- Discord or Slack for power users (opt-in, not forced) — becomes the testimonial engine
- "Beta milestone" announcements (10 integrations live; 50K memories stored; first 100 users crossing 30 days)

**Metrics for Stage 2:**

- Day 7 retention > 55%
- Day 30 retention > 30%
- NPS > 30 (target 40+)
- 20+ written testimonials captured
- 5+ case studies with real users who consented to be referenced
- Paid conversion intent: simulated paywall with 40%+ "would pay"

**Gate to Stage 3 launch:** metrics above sustained for 14 days; App Store approved; no high-severity trust incidents.

---

### Stage 3: Public launch (Week 20 → Week 24)

**Goal:** 10,000 paying or trialing users in the first 6 weeks; top-3 Product Hunt day; press cycle momentum.

**Launch day (singular moment):**

- **Product Hunt launch** at 12:01 AM PT on a Tuesday (highest-traffic day). Prepared assets: hero video, 5 screenshots, 3 micro-demos, launch comment from CEO explaining the "why now."
- **Hacker News Show HN.** Separate post, honest framing, CTO and CEO responding live for first 8 hours.
- **X thread from CEO.** 12-tweet narrative with screenshots and the core product demo.
- **LinkedIn post from CEO and early advisors.** Targeting exec personas.
- **Email to 10K waitlist.** "We're live. You're invited. Here's the price."
- **Press embargo break** — 5 tier-1 publications with pre-embargoed coverage (The Verge, TechCrunch, Wired, Fast Company, The Information). Each gets a different angle.

**Launch week (day 2–7):**

- CEO on 3–4 podcasts recorded pre-launch, live this week
- Daily "we shipped this" micro-posts
- Reply-all engagement on social: every comment, every question
- Referral program live — "give a month of Pro, get a month"

**Metrics for Stage 3:**

- Product Hunt: top 3 of the day
- Launch week signups: 10,000
- Launch week paid conversions: 3–5% (300–500 paying)
- Press pickup: 10+ quality articles
- Retention of launch-week signups at Day 7: 50%+

### Stage 4: Growth (Month 2 → Month 12)

**Goal:** 10K → 100K users, of whom 4–6% pay. Establish a repeatable growth loop that doesn't depend on founder-led PR.

This is where GTM shifts from "singular moments" to "compounding machinery." The machinery has three layers.

#### Layer A: Content flywheel (biggest lever)

**Content mission:** Be the single best source online for the question "how do I use AI to run my life/work better?"

**Pillar content formats:**

- **Weekly essay:** One 1,500-word essay on a real theme (e.g., "How I ran my week using an AI chief of staff"), distributed via our email and syndicated on LinkedIn/Medium.
- **YouTube channel:** 1 short-form per week (60–90 seconds), 1 medium-form per month (8–12 minutes). Visual demos, narrated.
- **Case studies:** Monthly deep-dive with a real user (Maya-type) showing how they use Aldrich. Real numbers, real workflows.
- **Prompt library / playbook:** SEO-anchored resource around jobs-to-be-done (e.g., "prep for a board meeting"). Each page demonstrates Aldrich capability.

**SEO targets (by Month 12):**

- Rank top 5 for 50 long-tail "AI chief of staff" / "personal AI assistant" terms
- Monthly organic traffic: 100K unique visitors
- Organic waitlist/signup contribution: 20% of new users

#### Layer B: Referral and community (second lever)

**Referral program:**

- Give a friend 1 month free Pro; get 1 month free yourself. Cap at 12 months/year.
- In-app share moment after a user experiences a "wow" (e.g., first successful multi-step action).
- Rewarded review program: 1 month Pro for an App Store review.

**Community:**

- Private Slack/Discord for paying users (aspirational, not chaotic)
- Monthly "Aldrich Live" — founders demo new features; users share workflows
- Ambassador program — top 50 users by advocacy get early access, swag, direct founder access

#### Layer C: Partnerships and PR (third lever)

**Creator partnerships:**

- Partnering with 20 mid-tier productivity and business creators (50K–500K followers each) on authentic usage reviews. Not sponsored posts in the typical sense — paid usage trials with honest write-ups, framed for the creator's audience.
- Target ROI per partnership: 200+ paid conversions in 30 days.

**Strategic partnerships:**

- Calendly / Notion / Linear / Slack / Todoist — co-marketing moments around integrations.
- Apple and Google developer relations — spotlight placements when features align with their platform events.
- Corporate wellness / exec coaching programs — prosumer placement into larger orgs without a full enterprise sale.

**PR rhythm:**

- Quarterly feature announcement with press outreach
- Monthly "by the numbers" — usage stats, memory size, actions taken — social proof via data
- Annual "State of the AI Assistant" report — thought leadership

---

### Stage 5: Scale (Year 2+)

Once we have 100K+ users, the GTM shifts again toward:

- **B2B prosumer expansion:** Maya brings it to her team. Team plans, not enterprise yet.
- **International expansion:** English-first, then Spanish, French, German localizations.
- **Enterprise wedge:** Via prosumer users asking their IT for official approval. Deliberate, slow, high-value.
- **Performance marketing:** Only if unit economics clear the bar. LTV:CAC must be > 4x before we spend a dollar on paid.

## Part 2: First 100 / 1K / 10K users plans

### First 100 users (Week 17–18 of build plan)

**Profile:** Hand-picked from waitlist. Personally invited by a founder. Mostly exec and founder persona.

**Execution:**

- CEO and CTO each personally invite 50 via direct email with a 1-line personal reason they were picked.
- Onboarding: concierge. Founder schedules 15-minute call with any user who wants it; captures feedback.
- Feedback loop: every Friday, a "here's what you said and what we fixed" email.

**Success criteria:**

- 80%+ of invites accept
- 70%+ active after Day 7
- 50%+ active after Day 30
- 10+ testimonials captured

### First 1,000 users (Week 18–19)

**Profile:** Waitlist invitees + referrals. 70% Maya, 20% Sam, 10% Jordan.

**Execution:**

- Automated invite waves of 200 at a time, every 3 days
- Onboarding: in-app only (concierge scaled out); founder email still sent to every new user on Day 1
- Feedback: in-app feedback widget; weekly digest reviewed by CEO and CPO

**Success criteria:**

- D7 retention > 55%
- D30 retention > 35%
- NPS > 40
- First 50 Pro-intent signals (from simulated paywall)

### First 10,000 users (Launch week + 4 weeks)

**Profile:** Open waitlist + Product Hunt + press. Broader persona mix.

**Execution:**

- Full funnel active: organic, paid (minimal), referral, press
- Onboarding at scale: automated 48h calibration flow; first-week nurture email sequence
- Support at scale: AI-first support for Free; human support within 4 hours for Pro/Power

**Success criteria:**

- 10,000 total users by end of Week 6
- 4–6% paid conversion
- D7 retention > 50%
- Unit economics tracking within forecast
- Zero high-severity trust incidents

## Part 3: Pricing strategy

### Pricing tiers

| Tier | Price | Who | What's included | Caps |
|------|-------|-----|-----------------|------|
| **Free** | $0 | Curious, students, low-intensity | Memory (1K facts), morning briefing, 5 actions/day, 1 integration | 1K facts, 5 actions/day, 1 integration, 3-day memory history |
| **Pro** | $18/month or $180/year | Maya, mainstream prosumer | Everything in Free + unlimited actions, all integrations, priority support, unlimited memory, full history | Reasonable fair-use caps only |
| **Power** | $35/month or $340/year | Sam, Jordan, heavy user | Everything in Pro + custom automations, API beta, team memory (up to 5), premium model access, early features | Unlimited for most purposes |

### Pricing rationale

**Why $18 for Pro (not $10 or $25)?**

- $10 anchors against ChatGPT Plus ($20) poorly — we look cheaper but weaker.
- $20 is the category expectation for AI productivity (ChatGPT, Notion AI, Copilot Pro).
- $18 signals "slightly better than the category expectation" — psychologically shorter than $20 and yearly math is clean ($216 vs $240).
- Margin after App Store 30% (or 15% small-business after Y1) and LLM costs still leaves $8–10 gross profit per user per month at 10K scale.

**Why $35 for Power?**

- Psychologically 2x of Pro — feels "serious tier."
- Captures the Sam persona who already pays for 5+ SaaS tools at $20–$50 each.
- Allows us to offer "premium" features that don't fit mainstream without diluting Pro.

**Why Free tier exists at all?**

- Acquisition engine: most sign-ups begin Free, convert after experiencing the value.
- Memory is the hook — once a user has given Aldrich 100 facts, switching cost is real.
- Lets us keep a large audience engaged with the brand (community, content, referrals).
- The caps are deliberate: 5 actions/day lets a user feel the value but need more within a week.

### Pricing tactics

- **Annual default with "2 months free" framing.** Doubles commitment, pre-pays LTV, reduces churn optics.
- **Student discount: 50% off Pro** with verified .edu email.
- **Referral: 1 month free for both sides** — no cash back (keeps economics clean; limits abuse).
- **Gift cards / gifting:** for Stage 4; viral mechanic around the holidays.
- **Team plans (Stage 4):** 5-seat pack at $75/month ($15/seat effective). Prosumer-to-team upsell path.

### What we will NOT do in V1

- **No usage-based pricing.** Too much cognitive load; hurts trust ("am I being charged for this?").
- **No lifetime deals.** Hurts LTV math; attracts wrong users.
- **No "forever free" promise of any feature.** We reserve the right to move capabilities between tiers based on cost and usage signal.
- **No ads in Free tier.** Breaks trust brand.
- **No selling of user data, ever.** Not a pricing lever.

### Pricing experiments (to run in beta and early GA)

- A/B test $15 vs $18 vs $22 for Pro (1K users per cohort, 30-day conversion window)
- Test "first month $5" vs "7-day free trial" for Pro acquisition
- Test Power positioning (prosumer vs. agency vs. "unlimited") — which frame converts best
- Test annual discount size (10% vs 17% vs 20%) on LTV

## Part 4: Unit economics targets

### Per-user economics at 10K-user scale

| Metric | Free | Pro | Power | Blended |
|--------|------|-----|-------|---------|
| Subscription revenue | $0 | $18 | $35 | $8.10 |
| App Store fee (30% Y1, 15% Y2+) | $0 | $5.40 | $10.50 | ~$2.70 |
| Net revenue | $0 | $12.60 | $24.50 | $5.40 |
| LLM cost | $0.80 | $3.40 | $6.00 | $1.90 |
| Infra cost (compute, storage) | $0.20 | $0.50 | $0.80 | $0.30 |
| Support cost allocation | $0.10 | $0.40 | $0.80 | $0.25 |
| **Gross profit** | -$1.10 | $8.30 | $16.90 | $2.95 |
| **Gross margin** | n/a | 66% | 69% | 55% |

*At 10K-user scale with 5% paid mix. Blended improves substantially at 100K scale as Free cost per user drops and paid mix grows.*

### LTV and CAC targets

- **Pro tier ARPU (year 1):** $150 (accounts for annual discount, churn)
- **Power tier ARPU:** $300
- **Blended monthly churn target:** 4% for Pro, 3% for Power
- **LTV (blended):** ~$220 (gross profit LTV over ~22 month avg tenure)
- **Target CAC:** < $50 (LTV:CAC > 4x)
- **Target payback period:** < 12 months

### CAC breakdown by channel target

| Channel | Year 1 target CAC | Scale feasibility |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| Organic (word-of-mouth, direct) | $0 | Scales with product quality |
| Content / SEO | $5–15 | Scales with content investment |
| Referral program | $15–25 | Depends on referral cap structure |
| Creator partnerships | $30–60 | Limited by creator pool |
| Press / PR | Not directly attributable | Amplifies other channels |
| Paid (search, social) | $50+ | Only unlock once LTV:CAC > 4x on other channels |

**Key rule:** no paid acquisition until organic and content channels prove LTV:CAC > 4x. Paid is an *amplifier* of a proven loop, not a discovery mechanism.

## Part 5: Go-to-market for B2B wedge (V2+)

While V1 is consumer-first, we set up the B2B wedge early to avoid a painful pivot later.

**B2B wedge thesis:** Maya is a VP of Product. If Aldrich earns her trust, she wants it for her team — not an enterprise deployment, just "my 5 PMs should each have this." That's a team plan, not a full enterprise sale.

**The path:**

1. **Team plan (Month 9–12):** 5-seat pack at $75/month; single billing; no admin console yet; shared team memory opt-in.
2. **SMB plan (Month 12–18):** 20-seat pack with admin console (usage, billing, SSO-lite); customizable team memory.
3. **Enterprise plan (Year 2+):** SSO, SCIM, custom DPAs, SOC 2 Type II, dedicated account manager. Long sales cycle, high ACV.

We do not build enterprise features in V1 because the complexity slows V1 down and most enterprise buyers won't commit to a startup with <1,000 paying users anyway.

## Part 8: Risks specific to GTM

- **Launch flops.** Mitigated by pre-launch audience building; even a bad launch produces 2–3K signups if waitlist is warm.
- **Viral coefficient never happens.** Mitigated by paid conversion being the primary revenue driver — virality is a bonus, not a prerequisite.
- **Press falls flat.** Mitigated by diversified channels — content, community, partnerships all running in parallel.
- **Paid conversion under-performs.** Mitigated by aggressive pricing experiments in beta before locking.
- **High churn masks growth.** Mitigated by focus on Day 30 retention as the north-star metric, not signups.
- **Messaging fragments.** Mitigated by a single-source-of-truth messaging doc (this one), reviewed before every campaign.

## Part 9: The "we will know GTM is working when..." list

- Waitlist converts to active at >40% when invited.
- Day 30 retention of launch cohort exceeds 30%.
- Organic signups exceed 30% of total by Month 6.
- At least one unprompted viral moment per quarter (a tweet thread, a podcast reference, a creator review).
- NPS >40 and at least 60% of NPS promoters have shared the product with someone.
- Three of every five new signups in Month 12 come from channels that didn't exist in Month 3 — i.e., our content and community flywheels are compounding.
- Paid conversion at 4%+ at 50K DAU.
- LTV:CAC > 4x before we spend a dollar on performance marketing.
