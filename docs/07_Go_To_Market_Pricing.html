Executive summary — the GTM thesis

Visible, daily value to a narrow but high-intensity persona.

Aldrich earns users by delivering visible, daily value to a narrow but high-intensity persona (the multi-threaded executive). They become organic promoters because the product makes their work life visibly better. We amplify with targeted content, founder-led PR, and a referral mechanic. We avoid paid acquisition until unit economics are proven.

Stage 1 — Pre-launch (Weeks 0–17)

Build a 10,000-person waitlist of intent-signaling users.

Simple 3-question form: role / biggest time-drain / email. Q1 segments invites by persona; Q2 gives real language for messaging.

  • Founder narrative on X + LinkedIn. Weekly posts from CEO + CTO about building Aldrich.
  • Newsletter placements. Lenny's, The Generalist, Every — sponsored slots.
  • Podcast circuit. CEO on 10–15 founder-targeted podcasts.
  • High-signal communities. Superpath, Lenny's, On Deck, Reforge alumni — value-first, no spam.
  • Public build log. Weekly updates → email + RSS → content flywheel for months.

Stage 1 metrics: 10K waitlist by W17 · 40%+ exec/manager/founder fit · 30%+ email open rate · referenced in ≥3 high-signal external conversations / month.

Stage 2 — Closed beta (Weeks 17–20)

200 → 5,000 active beta users with strong feature completion.

  • W17–18: 200 invites to hand-picked waitlist (best-fit personas, thoughtful answers).
  • W18–19: 1,000 invites prioritizing Maya-type execs and strong referral generators.
  • W19–20: Open to 5,000 with referral mechanic ("skip the waitlist by inviting 2 people").

What we do during beta: weekly founder email, in-app feedback tied to actions, 5–10 user interviews/week, opt-in Discord/Slack for power users, beta milestone announcements.

Stage 2 metrics

Day 7 retention > 55% · Day 30 > 30% · NPS > 30 (target 40+) · 20+ written testimonials · 5+ case studies · 40%+ "would pay" at simulated paywall.

Stage 3 — Public launch (Weeks 20–24)

Single-day moment. Prepared assets. Five tier-1 publications under embargo.

  • Product Hunt at 12:01am PT Tuesday. Hero video, 5 screenshots, 3 micro-demos, launch comment.
  • Hacker News Show HN. Honest framing. CTO + CEO live for the first 8 hours.
  • X thread from CEO — 12 tweets, screenshots, core demo.
  • LinkedIn from CEO + advisors targeting exec personas.
  • 10K waitlist email — "We're live. You're invited."
  • Press embargo break — 5 publications: Verge, TechCrunch, Wired, Fast Company, The Information. Each gets a different angle.

Stage 3 metrics: top-3 Product Hunt · 10K signups in launch week · 3–5% paid (300–500 paying) · 10+ quality articles · 50%+ Day-7 retention.

Aldrich · Go-to-Market & Pricing
Pricing rationale

Why $18 — not $10, not $25.

  • $10 anchors against ChatGPT Plus ($20) poorly — looks cheaper but weaker.
  • $20 is the category expectation (ChatGPT, Notion AI, Copilot Pro).
  • $18 signals "slightly better than the category expectation" — psychologically shorter than $20, and yearly math is clean ($216 vs. $240).
  • Margin after App Store 30% (or 15% small-business after Y1) and LLM costs leaves $8–10 gross profit per user/month at 10K scale.
Why Power at $35

Psychologically 2× of Pro — feels "serious tier." Captures Sam who already pays for 5+ SaaS tools at $20–$50. Allows premium features without diluting Pro.

Aldrich · Go-to-Market & Pricing
Why Free at all

Free is the acquisition engine — not the satisfaction tier.

  • Most signups begin Free, convert after experiencing value.
  • Memory is the hook — once a user has given Aldrich 100 facts, switching cost is real.
  • Lets us keep a large audience engaged with the brand (community, content, referrals).
  • Caps are deliberate: 5 actions/day lets a user feel value but need more within a week.

Aldrich · Go-to-Market & Pricing
Pricing tactics + don'ts

Annual default. No usage-based. No lifetime deals.

Tactics we use

  • Annual default with "2 months free" framing
  • Student discount: 50% off Pro (.edu verified)
  • Referral: 1 month free both sides (no cash)
  • Holiday gifting (Stage 4)
  • Team plans (Stage 4): 5-seat at $75 ($15/seat)

What we won't do in V1

  • Usage-based pricing — too much cognitive load
  • Lifetime deals — wrong users, hurts LTV
  • "Forever free" promises — reserve flexibility
  • Ads in Free — breaks trust brand
  • Sell user data — ever

Part 5 — B2B wedge for V2+

Maya brings it to her team. That's the wedge.

Not enterprise — team plans. Maya is a VP. If Aldrich earns her trust, she wants it for her team. "My 5 PMs should each have this."

StageMonthPlan
Team plan9–125-seat pack at $75/mo, single billing, no admin console, shared team memory opt-in
SMB plan12–1820-seat with admin console (usage, billing, SSO-lite); customizable team memory
EnterpriseY2+SSO, SCIM, custom DPAs, SOC 2 Type II, dedicated AM. Long sales cycle, high ACV

Don't build enterprise features in V1 — complexity slows V1 down, most enterprise buyers won't commit to a startup with <1,000 paying users anyway.

Part 9 — How we'll know GTM is working

Eight signals that tell us the engine is on.

  • Waitlist converts to active at > 40% when invited.
  • Day 30 retention of launch cohort exceeds 30%.
  • Organic signups exceed 30% of total by Month 6.
  • ≥1 unprompted viral moment per quarter.
  • NPS > 40 and ≥60% of promoters have shared with someone.
  • 3 of 5 new signups in Month 12 come from channels that didn't exist in Month 3.
  • Paid conversion ≥ 4% at 50K DAU.
  • LTV:CAC > 4× before we spend a dollar on performance marketing.
