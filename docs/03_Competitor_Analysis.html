Section 1 — The competitive landscape

Aldrich sits at the intersection of four overlapping markets.

  • Personal AI assistants — Siri, Google Assistant, Alexa, ChatGPT
  • Productivity software — Notion, Superhuman, Linear, Motion
  • Agentic AI / automation — MultiOn, Lindy, Adept, Zapier AI, Copilot
  • Capture / memory tools — Rewind, Mem.ai, Granola, Reflect, Bee

Aldrich's bet: the user who wants all four is a meaningful segment, and no single competitor serves them well. This deck validates or invalidates that bet.

Aldrich · Competitor Analysis
3.1 — ChatGPT (OpenAI)

Threat level: HIGH.

~200M weekly active, ~30M paying. Free / $20 Plus / $200 Pro. Memory added 2024 (still shallow). GPT Store integrations growing. Computer-Use beta = early agentic capability. Fundamentally reactive.

Why HIGH threat

OpenAI can bolt proactivity onto ChatGPT faster than any other company can build a comparable distribution channel. If they ship "ChatGPT that watches your Gmail," our addressable market halves overnight.

Aldrich's edge

Privacy architecture (local-first vs. data-flows-to-cloud), depth of integration (ChatGPT integrations are LCD), dedicated consumer mobile product purpose-built for daily orchestration.

3.2 — Microsoft Copilot

HIGH for Enterprise, MEDIUM for Consumer.

~50M Copilot Consumer; ~5M paid M365 seats. $20 Pro / $30 M365. Memory via Microsoft Graph + Recall. Deep M365 (Outlook, Teams, Word, Excel, OneDrive); weak elsewhere.

Aldrich's edge: The user who lives in Gmail + Slack + Notion + Linear is not well-served by Microsoft. That's 40–60% of knowledge workers.

Watch

Recall / Copilot Pro for consumers is the single Microsoft product that could encroach. If it matures, it's the most direct competitor in 2027.

3.3 — Apple Intelligence + next-gen Siri

Threat level: MEDIUM.

1.4B active iOS devices. Free with hardware. Apple-first integrations; limited third-party. Best privacy story.

But: 13-year track record of Siri disappointing, slow execution velocity, ecosystem lock-in means they can't serve the 60% of users who also live in non-Apple tools.

Aldrich's edge: Cross-ecosystem depth. Apple will build a better Siri. They will not integrate well with Slack, Notion, and Linear — doing so weakens their ecosystem pitch.

3.7 — Notion AI + Slack AI + Gmail Smart Compose

Threat level: COLLECTIVE HIGH.

Hundreds of millions combined users. Bundled into existing subscriptions. No single product is a direct competitor — but collectively they represent "death by a thousand good-enoughs."

Every productivity tool ships its own AI layer. The user never feels enough cross-tool pain to pay $18/mo for a separate layer.

Aldrich's edge: The user who lives in 4+ tools gets diminishing value from each individual AI layer and increasing value from a cross-tool layer. This user exists — and must be found.

Indirect substitutes

Where users send their pain instead.

  • Human EAs / Chiefs of Staff (Base Networks, Athena, Gloria) — $1,500–$5,000/mo. Real substitute for power users. Complementary, not competitive. 50–100× cheaper, 24/7. Their edge: judgment, social context.
  • Productivity stacks — Superhuman ($30, email), Motion ($19, calendar — closest competitor), Reclaim, Clockwise. Aldrich's edge: breadth of scope.
  • AI email clients — Shortwave, Spark. LOW threat — email-only doesn't solve cross-tool pain.
  • PKM tools — Reflect, Mem, Heptabase, Tana. LOW threat — authoring tools, not action tools.
  • AI hardware — Humane, Rabbit R1, Friend. VERY LOW. Form factor is wrong for knowledge work.

Section 9 — The three moats, in one breath

Three moats. Each compounds. Each is structurally hard for incumbents to replicate.

The next slide breaks them down with scoring. This one is the headline — what holds when an incumbent or a well-funded startup tries to take our position.

1. Memory

Compounds with time

Every interaction makes Aldrich a worse product to leave. After 6 months a user's switching cost is real and visible. After 12 months it's deep enough to cite to investors.

2. Privacy architecture

Structurally hard for Google

Google's ad business is incompatible with on-device + per-user-encrypted memory. Microsoft has a better shot but is enterprise-anchored. Apple is closest — but only on Apple-only workflows.

3. Cross-platform integration depth

Neutrality is the moat

Apple won't integrate well with Slack/Notion/Linear because that weakens the Apple ecosystem pitch. Microsoft is M365-locked. Aldrich is the only one structurally free to be neutral across all four major productivity stacks.

Why "compounds"

Each moat strengthens the others. Privacy architecture protects the memory. Memory rewards cross-platform integration. Cross-platform makes the privacy story credible. Lose one, and the other two erode within 12 months.

Section 11 — The fear list

Aldrich should fear pivots, not players.

Not any of the named companies. The thing to fear most: a well-executed pivot by a current indirect competitor.

  • Rewind.ai adding agentic action — already has context depth; needs action layer + approval UX.
  • Motion expanding beyond calendar — already has proactive AI positioning; needs broader integrations.
  • Superhuman adding cross-tool — has the premium user base and pricing; needs to escape email.
  • An ex-Apple/ex-Google founder with $10M+ seed and the same thesis — no product but huge distribution via network.

Each of these has 40–70% of the Aldrich build already done.

Section 13 — GTM takeaways from competitive analysis

Six rules from looking at the field.

  • Don't fight on distribution. Win by being the obvious best choice for a specific user actively looking.
  • Own a positioning nobody else has. "AI chief of staff" too vague. "The AI that handles your inbox" — owned and specific.
  • Compete on trust, not features. Few competitors will invest in approval UX, undo, compensating transactions, safety layer.
  • Partner instead of fight. Slack, Notion, Linear would rather see Aldrich win than Google/MS absorb the workflow.
  • Plan for ChatGPT to bolt on proactivity in 2026–2027. Don't panic. Double down on cross-ecosystem power-users.
  • Treat year 1 as trust + category-education. Goal isn't to beat Siri — it's to be the obvious choice for the 5% actively searching.
