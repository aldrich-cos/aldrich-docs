How to read this register

Every risk has six fields.

  • Likelihood — Very Low (<5%) / Low (5–20%) / Medium (20–50%) / High (50–80%) / Very High (>80%) over 18 months.
  • Impact — Cosmetic / Minor / Major / Severe / Existential.
  • Risk Score — qualitative likelihood × impact, reviewed monthly.
  • Trigger — the observable signal that says it's happening now.
  • Mitigation — prevention before the trigger fires.
  • Contingency — response when the trigger fires.
  • Owner — a named individual (by role), not a team.

Risk #1 — Platform · CRITICAL

iOS always-on listening is not feasible.

Likelihood: Very High · Impact: Severe · Owner: CTO

iOS enforces orange mic indicator, kills background audio after short windows, requires foreground for any practical mic use. Any workaround is grounds for App Store rejection.

  • Mitigation. Reframe from "omnipresent" → "present on demand." Build around push-triggered moments: location arrival, calendar briefings, notification interception. On Android, exercise foreground service carefully — same "user feels in control" philosophy. Update every external piece of copy before V1.
  • Contingency. Proactively correct any reviewer/investor under that misperception. With users: explain the OS constraint and demonstrate the alternative trigger-based design.

Aldrich · Risk Register
Risk #3 — Platform · HIGH

App Store / Play Store rejection during launch.

Likelihood: Medium (30–40% first submission) · Impact: Severe · Owner: CEO + CTO

  • Triggers. Guideline 2.5.1 (OS functionality), 4.0 (design), or 5.1.1 (privacy) cited. 14+ days in review. Apple/Google policy update implicating assistant apps.
  • Mitigation. Pre-submission Apple Office Hours. Scope V1 to least-OS-intrusive feature set. No private APIs. Pair every permission request with plain-English in-app explanation. Use Apple's own Intents/Shortcuts. Attach 2-min screen recording.
  • Contingency. Day 1: read rejection carefully, don't re-submit. Day 2: appeal or fix. Day 3–5: minimum change + detailed explanation. Two same-grounds rejections → request live call. Three → pivot feature out of V1, ship without it.

Risk #6 — Platform · CRITICAL

Apple or Google ships a first-party equivalent.

Likelihood: High (60–80% over 24 months) · Impact: Severe · Owner: CEO

  • Triggers. WWDC / Google I/O announcements of personal agents, cross-app memory, proactive suggestions. Multi-step Siri demos. Public on-device LLM models for developers.
  • Mitigation. Build where platforms won't — cross-platform, non-native integrations (Notion, Slack, Linear, Airtable, Jira). Own "your data, your agent, not Apple's." Integrate with OS primitives where available. Maintain a 6-month feature runway ahead of incumbents on proactive behavior. Build B2B/prosumer use cases.
  • Contingency. Same week of announcement: blog post on the difference. Email blast to existing users. Board within 2 weeks. If feature overlap >60% → pivot to narrower wedge (B2B or specific vertical).

Risk #14 — Platform · HIGH

iOS Shortcuts / App Intents deprecated or throttled.

Likelihood: Low (10–20%) · Impact: Severe · Owner: CTO

  • Triggers. Deprecation in iOS release notes. Rate limits on Shortcuts execution. App Intents API surface shrinks.
  • Mitigation. Don't rely on a single automation path — every cross-app action has a fallback (Shortcut → native SDK → web/API → manual). Maintain a swappable ActionAdapter abstraction. Be visible in Apple's developer program.
  • Contingency. Pivot affected features to next-best path. Communicate transparently. Accelerate web/desktop surface if iOS becomes too hostile.

Risk #2 — AI · CRITICAL

LLM reliability at long tool chains (<80%).

Likelihood: High (60–70% if we attempt long chains) · Impact: Severe · Owner: Head of AI

97% per-step success → 8-step chain ~22% failure end-to-end. No user trusts that twice.

  • Mitigation. Cap V1 chain depth at 3 without explicit approval. Approval gate on every non-reversible action. Classifier routes deterministic intents to traditional code; reasoning to LLM. Retry with decay. 200+ realistic eval set, run weekly. Circuit breaker on 3 user-visible errors per session.
  • Contingency. Tighten approval gates (promote optional → mandatory). Reduce chain depth cap globally; communicate as "quality improvement." Invest in a planner→critic model.

Risk #4 — AI · HIGH

Prompt injection / jailbreak in production.

Likelihood: High (70%+ attempts; varies success) · Impact: Major to Severe · Owner: Head of Security

  • Mitigation (defense in depth). Content-role separation — external content wrapped in "untrusted" tags. First-pass classifier flags imperatives. Hard-coded allowlist of destructive actions (separate non-LLM policy engine). Biometric gate for high-trust. Memory-write requires explicit user tap. Continuous red team + bug bounty within 6 months. 30-day undo stack.
  • Contingency. Hour 1: kill switch on the action class. Hour 2–24: incident response, notify affected, log, estimate blast radius. 24–72h: public postmortem. Add vector to eval harness.

Aldrich · Risk Register
Risk #13 — AI · HIGH

Memory drift & contradiction accumulation.

Likelihood: High (70%+ over 18 months) · Impact: Major · Owner: Head of AI

Long-tenured users accumulate contradictions: old job, closed coffee shop, wrong birthday. Retrieval quality degrades — the assistant feels dumb precisely for the highest-value users.

  • Mitigation. Fact provenance (source, timestamp, user-confirmation). Recency bias in retrieval. Background contradiction detector → surface to user. Monthly memory review UI. Forgetting curve. User-pinnable "canonical" memories.
  • Contingency. User-facing memory cleanup flow. Emergency retrieval-weight adjustment. Long-term: structured schema for high-value entities (employer, location, spouse, routines) with explicit "current" vs "historical" fields.

Risks #15 + #24 — AI · HIGH / HIGH

Over-automation backlash. On-device LLM quality plateau.

#15 — Over-automation

Users say they want automation; the product can cross a line. "Why did you just send that?" is a churn moment.

Mitigation: Default to suggest, not act. Transparent logs. Dial of control (Ask everything → Money/people only → Low-stakes trust → Full autopilot). Default = middle. Celebration of restraint.

#24 — On-device plateau

If small models stall <15% below cloud, we either pay cloud costs for everything (bad UE) or ship a worse experience.

Mitigation: Router scopes on-device to well-defined tasks. Reasoning + generation stays on cloud. Quarterly evals of new on-device models (Phi, Llama-small, Gemma variants).

Risk #5 — Financial · CRITICAL

Runway exhaustion before PMF.

Likelihood: Medium (30–40%) · Impact: Existential · Owner: CEO/CFO

  • Triggers. Cash runway <9 months. Burn multiple (net burn ÷ net new ARR) >3×. Past M9 with no term sheet.
  • Mitigation. Raise $4M, plan $3M, spend $2.5M (25% buffer). Monthly burn discipline with hard +10% variance stop. Milestone-gated hires. Revenue-generating beta at M6 not M12. Stage the fundraise.
  • Contingency. 9mo runway: freeze hiring, cut discretionary, accelerate raise. 6mo: layoffs of non-essentials, cash-generating features. 3mo: bridge or down-round; consider acquihire in parallel. 1mo: notify board, avoid bad deals under duress.

Risk #10 — Financial · HIGH

LLM unit economics inversion.

Likelihood: Medium (30%) · Impact: Severe · Owner: CFO/CTO

  • Triggers. Avg LLM cost/user > $5/mo. Power-user (top 5%) > $25/mo. Anthropic announces >20% price change.
  • Mitigation. Multi-provider (Claude/GPT/open-source via Bedrock). Aggressive caching for deterministic sub-responses. Token-budget enforcement: Free 10K/day, Pro 100K, Power 300K. Volume-tier contracts with advance-notice clauses.
  • Contingency. Increase Free rate limits → nudge to paid. Raise prices with 60-day notice as "investment in quality." Accelerate on-device handling.

Risks #17 + #11 — Financial · CRITICAL / HIGH

Series A unraisable. Weak paid conversion.

#17 — Can't raise A

Existential. No term sheets 6 weeks in. Investor feedback clusters around retention, conversion, LTV gap.

Mitigation: Retention-first product (D1, D7, D30 healthy before raising). Cohort-over-cohort LTV:CAC story. CEO maintains quarterly updates with ~25 Series A investors from seed close.

Contingency: Bridge from existing investors. Pivot to efficient GTM (B2B). Smaller A at lower valuation rather than no A. Worst case: lifestyle business at $5–10M ARR.

#11 — Weak conversion

Severe. Trial→paid <15% after 30 days. NPS high but conversion low = "love it but wouldn't pay."

Mitigation: A/B price points ($8/$15/$25/$35) in first 1K cohort. Anchor upgrade prompts to pain-moments. Annual discount. Team plans. Feature-gated premium.

Contingency: Re-test pricing $10/$25. "Pay what you think" tier for learning. Pivot to B2B where WTP is higher.

Risk #7 — Trust · CRITICAL

First public trust incident.

Likelihood: High (80%+ over 18 months — something will go wrong publicly) · Impact: Severe if mishandled, Minor if handled well · Owner: CEO + Head of Safety

  • Mitigation. Pre-written incident playbooks, owners defined, comms drafted. Transparency by default — every action logged, user-visible, reversible. "Restraint over reach" PRD principle: every PRD asks "what's the worst this can do?" User pre-flight on risky actions makes incidents easier to explain ("user approved").
  • Contingency. Hour 1: acknowledge to affected user. Hour 2: incident channel (CEO, Safety, CX, Comms). Hour 4: decision on user-base-wide comms (default yes). Day 1: public postmortem if >100 users or >1 tweet. Day 3: product fix deployed. Day 7: all affected users contacted with explanation + remedy.

Aldrich · Risk Register
Risk #16 — Security · CRITICAL

Data breach (memory leaked).

Likelihood: Low (5–10% over 24 months) · Impact: Existential · Owner: Head of Security

Aldrich's memory contains the most sensitive data a user has. A breach isn't 500M credentials on HIBP — it's "your life story is on 4chan."

  • Mitigation. Encryption at rest + in transit (non-negotiable). Per-user encryption keys held in user-controlled keychain. Least-privilege access internally — no engineer touches raw memory except via audited break-glass. SOC 2 Type II within 12 months; ISO 27001 within 24. Quarterly external pentest. Bug bounty before 10K paying users. Zero-knowledge for sensitive memory classes by Year 2.
  • Contingency. H 0–2: containment. H 2–12: scope assessment. H 12–72: GDPR/CCPA notifications (72h SLA). Week 1: public + individual disclosure. Offer credit monitoring, consider lifetime free service for affected users.

GDPR/EU AI Act. IP litigation. Forced regulated-AI status.

Risks #9, #19, #25 — Integrations, Vendor, Support

API breakage. Anthropic lock-in. Support load.

  • #9 — API integration breakage cascade (Very High / Minor each, Major cumulative). Synthetic test calls every 15 min per integration. Versioned adapters spun up side-by-side. Graceful degradation ("Can't reach Gmail right now — retry?"). V1 limits to top-10 services.
  • #19 — Vendor lock-in / Anthropic pricing (Medium / Major). Provider abstraction layer — model swap is config-only. Shadow-test 10% of traffic on backup provider continuously. Volume-tier contracts with notice periods. Diversify intent classification to cheaper open models from day one.
  • #25 — Support load explodes (High / Minor each, Major cumulative). In-app "explain that action" button reduces tickets. Self-service memory + action logs. Tiered: AI-first → human-second → premium response for Power. Content + docs preempt common Qs.

Risks #12 + #20 — People

Key-person dependency. AI-talent shortage.

#12 — Key person leaves

Medium / Major. Triggers: sole owner of a critical system, single on-call without backup, visible burnout.

Mitigation: Pair programming + knowledge shares. Runbooks for every key task. 4-yr vesting w/ cliff, accelerated on change of control. Cross-training rotation. Bench depth: 2+ engineers per critical system within 6 months.

#20 — Can't hire AI talent

Medium / Major. Senior AI engineers in extreme demand; can't compete on FAANG comp.

Mitigation: Mission + equity story (consumer AI is preferred by mission-driven engineers). 0.5–2% equity for strong early hires. Remote-friendly global hiring. CEO spends 30% of Year-1 time recruiting.

Incumbent bundling. Voice-first UX rejection.

Process

How we review this register.

Monthly. Quarterly. Annual.

27
Aldrich · Risk Register
Review cadence

Three rituals — none of them optional.

  • Monthly review. Exec team reviews dashboard at month-start. Any risk with score change is discussed. Triggers fired in prior month → contingency post-mortem. New risks added; outdated archived. Update version, circulate to board.
  • Quarterly deep-dive. Pick the three highest-score risks. 60-min team session: "If this fires next month, what's our actual response?" Stress-test the contingency.
  • Annual register re-baseline. Board process — zero-based recreate. Don't just edit last year's. Compare to catch what we've unconsciously dropped.
